Techniques used:

  • Divide & Conquer :
    Divide and Conquer is an algorithmic strategy that breaks a problem into smaller subproblems, solves them recursively, and then combines their solutions.
    Divide city into zones and optimize routing zone-wise.
  • Greedy Technique:
    The Greedy technique is an algorithmic approach that makes the locally optimal choice at each step.
    Choose lowest-cost next tower while laying fiber (Prim/Kruskal)

  • Backtracking:
    Backtracking is an algorithmic technique used to solve problems by exploring all possible solutions step by step and undoing (backtracking) choices when they lead to a dead end.
    When path fails, find alternative routing path.

Principles used:

  • Brave & Cautious Travel (DFS & BFS)
    DFS (Depth‚ÄëFirst Search) explores paths deeply, like a brave traveler venturing far before backtracking. BFS (Breadth‚ÄëFirst Search) explores level by level, cautiously covering all nearby nodes before moving outward.

  • Pruning:
    Pruning means cutting off unnecessary branches in a search tree. It avoids exploring paths that cannot lead to a valid or optimal solution.

  • Edge Relaxation: - Edge relaxation updates the shortest path estimate between two vertices. It‚Äôs the key step in algorithms like Dijkstra‚Äôs and Bellman‚ÄëFord.

Design Techniques used :
  • Decomposition :
    The incubator system is divided into smaller modules such as startup onboarding, mentorship management, resource allocation, funding evaluation, and performance reporting. This makes the system easier to design and manage.

  • Greedy Technique :
    A greedy approach is used while allocating limited resources such as funding, office space, and demo day slots by always selecting the highest-priority or best-performing startups first using a priority queue (heap).

  • Transform and Conquer :
    Startup performance data is transformed into sorted lists and ranked structures to make evaluation and decision-making faster and simpler.

  • Space‚ÄìTime Tradeoff :
    Hash tables are used to achieve fast lookup of startup and mentor information by using extra memory, trading space for time efficiency.

  • Decrease and Conquer :
    During application filtering, weak or incomplete startup applications are removed step-by-step, reducing the dataset and improving processing efficiency.


Principles used :
  • Pruning :
    Unqualified or duplicate startup applications are eliminated early during screening, reducing unnecessary processing and saving resources.

  • Pre-Computing :
    Historical startup performance metrics are pre-computed and stored using sparse tables so that reports and analytics can be generated instantly when required.

  • Hierarchical Data :
    The incubator follows a hierarchical structure such as Incubator ‚Üí Program ‚Üí Startup ‚Üí Team, enabling efficient organization and management of data.

  • Parental Dominance :
    The heap (priority queue) maintains the invariant that the parent node always has higher priority than its children, ensuring correct startup ranking for funding and selection decisions.

  • Invariants :
    System rules such as unique startup IDs and fixed funding criteria remain unchanged throughout execution, ensuring consistency and correctness.

  • Partitioning :
    Startups are partitioned based on domain, stage, or performance category to simplify evaluation and reporting processes.
